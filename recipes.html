Ghanaian Recipes

Discover the flavors of Ghana with these traditional recipes. Each dish is prepared with fresh, local ingredients.

Jollof Rice

A flavorful one-pot rice dish with tomatoes and spices!

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups long-grain rice
  • 4 tomatoes, blended
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp ginger, grated
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • Salt and spices to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a pot and saut√© onions, garlic, and ginger.
  2. Add blended tomatoes and cook until thickened.
  3. Add rice, water, and spices. Simmer until rice is cooked.
  4. Serve hot.

Banku

A fermented corn dough, often served with soup!

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cornmeal
  • Water
  • Salt

Instructions:

  1. Mix cornmeal with water to form a paste.
  2. Stir continuously over heat until thickened.
  3. Serve with soup or stew.

Waakye

Rice cooked with beans and served with spicy sauce!

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rice
  • 1/2 cup black-eyed peas
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • Spices and oil

Instructions:

  1. Soak and cook beans.
  2. Add rice and spices, cook together.
  3. Serve with sauce.

Fufu

A starchy side dish made from cassava and plantain!

Ingredients:

  • 2 cassava tubers
  • 2 plantains

Instructions:

  1. Boil cassava and plantains.
  2. Mash together until smooth.
  3. Serve with soup.

Kenkey

Fermented corn dough, a staple in Ghanaian cuisine!

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups cornmeal
  • Water
  • Salt

Instructions:

  1. Ferment cornmeal mixture.
  2. Steam in leaves until cooked.
  3. Serve with fish or stew.
